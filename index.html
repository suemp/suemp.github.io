Group Project: HBX (Husky Book X-change)
Type: Human Computer Interaction, UI/UX Design
Date: Autumn 2014


Background: College students attempt to find the least expensive option for obtaining their textbooks every quarter. The current options for this are the University bookstore or some online retailer such as Amazon.com or Half.com. Purchasing books through a retailer is the most expensive option. Discounted books through an online retailer require shipping, the student may have to wait weeks to receive purchased books.
Aim: Our aim is to introduce a network where students can connect with each other directly to buy, sell, and exchange books with ease.
Method: We plan to design a mobile application called the Husky Book X-change. This app will allow student to post their used school texts in order to sale or exchange them with other UW students.
Results: Give students a seamless option for purchasing textbooks at lower than retail cost, or to exchange with another student.
Conclusion: Our app will easily connect students to cut textbook costs. Students who have textbooks that they would like to sale or trade could use the site to connect with students who need to buy or trade textbooks. This would result in savings for all parties involved.
